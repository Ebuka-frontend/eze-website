The Art of Watchmaking

Crafted by Hand,
Measured in Lifetimes

Each Chronos timepiece requires over 400 hours of meticulous hand-assembly by master watchmakers in our Geneva atelier. From the first turned component to the final waterproof test, every step reflects a tradition of excellence passed down through four generations.

Our movements are developed entirely in-house — a rare distinction in modern horology. Each caliber is assembled, adjusted, and tested over several weeks to meet our exacting standards of precision, achieving chronometric certification that places Chronos among the world's finest.

Our Legacy

A Century of Innovation

1905

The Foundation

Henri Chronos establishes the manufacture in Geneva, Switzerland, with a vision to create timepieces of unparalleled precision and enduring beauty.

1926

The Waterproof Revolution

Introduction of the Oyster-case sealing system, making Chronos one of the first waterproof wristwatches in history — a breakthrough that changed horology forever.

1953

Conquering the Deep

The Mariner diving watch accompanies the first expedition to descend below 1,000 meters, proving its legendary water resistance under extreme conditions.

1988

The Perpetual Movement

Development of the in-house Cal. 3100 perpetual movement with 72-hour power reserve, setting a new standard for mechanical precision and reliability.

2024

The New Era

Unveiling of the next-generation Cal. 4000 series with silicon escapement, magnetic resistance to 15,000 gauss, and 120-hour power reserve — defining the future of Swiss watchmaking.
